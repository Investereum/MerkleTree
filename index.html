Frequently Asked Questions

The Merkle Tree Securities helps businesses turning their ICO's into STO's or set up a STO in the first place.

01. What is a utility token

A token that represents a unit of value that is used to purchase of goods or services within an ICO project’s ecosystem.

02. What is a security token?

A token that represents a tradeable financial asset. Typically in the form of debt, equity, or derivatives. In this context we are referring to equity.

03. Why are projects transitioning from utility to security tokens?

There are 2 broad reasons:

  • Token economics

    • Many ICO founders failed to fully understand the role their new token would play in their future business. For many it was an after-thought.
    After the funds were raised, a common theme emerged across the sector; the lack of liquidity, and the volatility of their native token created a huge impediment to their business.
    Put simply; ICO tokens are not suitable when it comes to paying for goods or services.



  • Legal
    • Recent regulatory developments have also proved a catalyst for ICO projects.
    Of particular note is the SEC’s statement in November 2018 when reaching a settlement with ICO projects Airfox and Paragon.

      “there is a path to compliance with the federal securities laws going forward, even where issuers have conducted an illegal unregistered offering of digital asset securities”


    When read together with the SEC chairman’s previous statement that “ every ICO I've seen is a security” - A number of projects have decided to take the prudent step of transitioning from utility to security.
    Our global network of legal practitioners has indicated that regulators throughout the OECD are likely to follow the SEC lead.
    This means that even projects who did not sell to US residents may wish to transition.

    04. What is involved in the conversion process?

    Issuing a security token requires a number of legal and regulatory steps that are not present when launching an ICO.
    This includes, but is not limited to:


    • Setting up a company in a jurisdiction which supports STOs
    • Drafting a prospectus
    • Obtaining regulatory approval
    • Undergoing KYC / AML checks

    In addition, participants will be required send their utility tokens to a specified smart contract address. Upon receipt of the utility tokens, new security tokens representing equity in the company will be issued by the smart contract.

    05. Are KYC / AML checks mandatory?

    Yes, as part of the registration process, you will be asked to undergo a KYC / AML check. Detailed instructions and information required will be made available during the token swap process.

    06. Will my rights change after swapping tokens

    Yes. As a security token holder you will gain rights and benefits associated with being an equity holder. For example, you will obtain an ownership stake in the company and be eligible for dividend payments.

    However, you will also lose certain rights as a utility token holder. I.e. sacrificing the ability to pay for and access certain services in the ICO’s eco-system.

    07. In which Jurisdiction will the swap take place?

    ICO projects will be required to establish a new holding company in Liechtenstein. This company will take the form of a special purpose vehicle (SPV). Created for the purpose of taking ownership of the existing company and allocating its equity via the STO process.

    08. Why Liechtenstein?

    Liechtenstein is one of the few countries in the world that has drafted and passed legislation governing the issuance of security tokens.

    09. Can residents of any country participate in the token swap?

    Currently we have restrictions on the following countries; Afghanistan, Bosnia And Herzegovina, China, Democratic People's Republic of Korea (DPRK), Ethiopia, Iran, Iraq, Pakistan, Syria, Sri Lanka, Trinidad and Tobago, Tunisia, Uganda, Yemen. Depending on the project we may also have restrictions on the United states of America.

    10. Is there a minimum amount of utility tokens that I can exchange for security tokens?

    No, however, each individual will be required to undergo KYC checks. Which, depending on the project, may involve a small processing fee.

    11. Is there any are conditions on those wishing to participate in the token swap?

    Standard KYC procedures will apply. Depending on the project and their jurisdiction, additional requirements will also apply.

    12. Is there a fee for swapping the tokens?

    This is up to the individual project. There are costs associated with the transition process. And the project founders can chose one of 3 options to cover the costs:

    • Use existing funds raised previously
    • Opt for a self funding model where users pay a small fee
    • A hybrid approach

    13. Where can I store the new tokens?

    Tokens can be stored in offline hardware wallets similar to their ERC 20 utility tokens.

    14. What block will the new tokens be issued on?

    Ethereum.

    15. What standard will the new tokens adhere to?

    There are currently a number of competing standards. We will liaise with each project to determine the appropriate standard to use.

    16. What happens if I don’t exchange my tokens?

    You will still retain your utility tokens.

    17. Will the new tokens be tradable on a securities exchange?

    We offer projects assistance with listing their new security token on a fully compliant security exchange.
